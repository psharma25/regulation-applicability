Threat actor reputation/board

Ranks ransomware & extortion actors on two axes — how dangerous they are, and whether they honor a payment — from analyst priors enriched with live public reporting.

View on GitHub Static snapshot — preloaded roster
This is the static showcase. It displays the preloaded roster with scores computed from analyst priors, so it runs anywhere (including GitHub Pages) with no backend. The full agentic scan — live evidence collection, new-actor discovery, local-LLM reasoning, and Excel export — runs in the Python app: pip install -r requirements.txt then uvicorn app.main:app, and open the dashboard at localhost:8000.
Threat severity — higher is worse for you Deal reliability — higher means more likely to honor payment State-directed — sanctions risk on payment

Scores are a planning aid, not an endorsement — every listed actor is a criminal operation. Ransoms actually paid and payer identities are mostly undisclosed; per-group decryptor success rates are tracked privately by negotiation firms. Reliability reflects reputation and incentive structure, not a verified success rate. Across all groups roughly a third of victims who pay receive non-working decryptors.
