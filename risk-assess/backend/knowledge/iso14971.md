# ISO 14971:2019 — Medical Device Risk Management

ISO 14971 is the international standard for application of risk management to medical devices. It defines a risk management process across the total product life cycle.

## Core definitions
Harm is injury or damage to the health of people, or damage to property or the environment. Hazard is a potential source of harm. Hazardous situation is a circumstance in which people, property or the environment are exposed to one or more hazards. Risk is the combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm.

## Risk model
Risk is estimated from two components: severity of harm and probability of occurrence of harm. The probability of occurrence of harm (P) can be decomposed into P1, the probability of a hazardous situation occurring, and P2, the probability that the hazardous situation leads to harm. Overall P = P1 x P2.

## Process steps
The process comprises: risk management plan; risk analysis (intended use, identification of hazards and hazardous situations, estimation of risk); risk evaluation against acceptability criteria; risk control; evaluation of overall residual risk; risk management review; and production and post-production monitoring.

## Risk control option priority
Risk controls must be applied in this order of priority: (1) inherently safe design and manufacture; (2) protective measures in the device itself or in manufacturing; (3) information for safety (labeling, instructions for use, training). Information for safety alone is the least preferred control and may not be used to justify acceptability where design controls are feasible.

## Residual and overall risk
After controls, residual risk is re-estimated. If residual risk is judged not acceptable, a benefit-risk analysis is performed: if the medical benefit outweighs the residual risk the risk may be acceptable, otherwise the risk remains unacceptable. After all individual risks are controlled, the overall residual risk of the device must be evaluated against criteria in the risk management plan, again using benefit-risk analysis.

## Note on FMEA / RPN
ISO 14971 does not require Risk Priority Numbers (RPN = Severity x Occurrence x Detection). It cautions against using detectability to lower the acceptability of a risk. RPN may be used for prioritization but not as the acceptability decision.

## Related guidance
ISO/TR 24971 provides guidance on applying ISO 14971, including example hazards, P1/P2 estimation, and risk acceptability criteria. IEC 62304 covers software life-cycle and software safety classification (Class A/B/C). IEC 62366-1 covers usability engineering and use-related risk.
