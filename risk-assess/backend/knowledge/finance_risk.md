# Finance Risk Management — ISO 31000, COSO ERM, Basel

## Frameworks
ISO 31000:2018 is the generic risk-management framework: it defines principles, a framework (leadership, integration, design, implementation, evaluation, improvement) and a process (scope/context/criteria, risk assessment = identification + analysis + evaluation, risk treatment, monitoring, communication). COSO ERM (2017, Enterprise Risk Management — Integrating with Strategy and Performance) frames risk in terms of governance, strategy, performance, review and information. The Basel Accords (Basel II/III) govern banking capital adequacy and define risk categories and capital charges.

## Risk taxonomy
Credit risk: loss from a borrower or counterparty failing to meet obligations; quantified via PD (probability of default), LGD (loss given default) and EAD (exposure at default). Expected Loss = PD x LGD x EAD. Market risk: loss from movements in interest rates, FX, equity or commodity prices; measured with Value at Risk (VaR), sensitivities (DV01, duration) and stress tests. Liquidity risk: inability to meet obligations as they fall due; monitored via LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio) and NSFR (Net Stable Funding Ratio). Operational risk: loss from failed internal processes, people, systems or external events (includes fraud, cyber, legal). Compliance/regulatory risk: penalties and remediation from breaching laws/regulations (e.g., AML/KYC). Strategic risk: loss from poor business decisions or failure to adapt.

## Scoring
Qualitative score = Likelihood x Impact (each 1-5). Quantitative overlay: Expected Annual Loss = Financial Exposure ($) x Annual Probability (%). Inherent risk is assessed before controls; residual risk after controls. Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) such as NPL ratio, LCR, DV01 limit breaches and SAR backlog are used as early-warning triggers.

## Risk appetite
A risk appetite statement defines the level of risk the organization will accept in pursuit of objectives. Risks above appetite require treatment (avoid, reduce, transfer/insure, or accept with justification).
